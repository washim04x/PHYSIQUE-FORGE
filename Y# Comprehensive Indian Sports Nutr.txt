## Part A: Indian Food Macronutrient Database

We compiled nutrient values for each listed food (per 100 g raw or dry weight) and a representative serving. Macronutrients (calories, protein, carbs, fat, fiber) and glycemic index (GI) are given. Cooking methods generally *do not change the total macronutrient content*, but water absorption and textural changes mean that cooked weights yield lower per-100 g values than raw. For example, **moong dal** (green gram): raw (100 g) ≈ **347 kcal, 24 g protein, 62 g carbs, 1 g fat, 16 g fiber**; cooked (100 g) ≈ **105 kcal, 7 g protein, 19 g carbs, 0.4 g fat, 6 g fiber**, GI ≈ 42. Below, tables summarize values (per 100 g raw/dry, and per common serving).  *GI values* are from published charts.  (Cooking notes: e.g. 100 g uncooked rice yields about 300 kcal; when boiled to 300 g cooked, ~130 kcal per 100 g. Oils and ghee are mostly fat; roasting legumes slightly raises glycemic response compared to soaking.)

## Part B: Budget-Stratified Meal Plans

We constructed daily meal frameworks for Indian diets under five budget brackets (₹ per day). For each, we list: achievable total calories, maximum protein, recommended foods, a sample 6-meal day, and a cost breakdown. Kolkata prices (July 2026) are used. Prices: moong dal ~₹115/kg, masoor dal ~₹108/kg, toor dal ~₹130/kg; rice ~₹60/kg, wheat flour ~₹35/kg, eggs ~₹6/pc, chicken ~₹200/kg, seasonal veg ~₹30–40/kg. 

### 1. ₹30–50/day (₹900–1,500/month) – Extreme Student Budget

- **Staples:** Cheap pulses (moong dal, masoor dal), rice, seasonal vegetables, minimal oil.  
- **Proteins:** Mostly plant: dal, + 1 egg if possible (extra ₹6).  
- **Calories:** ~600–800 kcal/day.  
- **Protein:** ~30–35 g/day max.  
- **Food stack:** Moong/masoor dal, rice or roti, potato/onion/tomato vegetables, eggs (if budget).  
- **Sample 6-meal plan:**  
  1. **Early Breakfast:** 30 g moong dal porridge (soaked overnight; cooked in tea-kettle) ~200 kcal, 12 g protein.  
  2. **Snack 1:** 1 small apple (~60 kcal) or 1 egg (78 kcal, 6 g protein, ₹6).  
  3. **Lunch:** 100 g cooked rice with 50 g cooked masoor dal ~200 kcal + 12 g protein. Add 50 g cooked seasonal veg.  
  4. **Snack 2:** 1 boiled egg or 30 g roasted chana (100 kcal, 5 g protein).  
  5. **Evening:** 2 small rotis (60 g flour, ~210 kcal, 6 g protein) with 50 g cooked dal or 50 g paneer bhurji (~150 kcal, 9 g protein) if affordable.  
  6. **Night:** 100 ml skim milk (35 kcal, 3.4 g protein) or curd (50 g, 30 kcal, 2 g protein).  

- **Budget breakdown (≈₹40):** 
  - Moong dal 150 g (₹17) → 522 kcal, 36 g protein.  
  - Rice 100 g (₹6) → 130 kcal.  
  - Wheat flour 100 g (₹3.5) → 299 kcal, 8 g protein (makes ~3 small rotis).  
  - Egg 1 (₹6) → 78 kcal, 6 g protein.  
  - Veg (tomato/onion/potato) 150 g (₹6) → ~75 kcal, 2 g protein.  
  - Oil/ghee 10 g (₹2) → 90 kcal.  
  - **Total:** ~49 kcal exceeds ₹50, but can trim portion slightly.  

*(This yields roughly 800–900 kcal and ~40 g protein.)*  

### 2. ₹50–100/day (₹1,500–3,000/month) – Moderate Student Budget

- **Staples:** Pulses, rice/wheat, eggs (2–3/day), seasonal veg, potato.  
- **Protein:** Aim for 50–60 g/day by adding eggs, paneer (if possible) or soya chunks.  
- **Calories:** ~1,200–1,500 kcal/day.  
- **Food stack:** Continue moong/masoor/chana dal, mix of rice and chapati, 2–3 eggs, peanuts or sattu for snacks, leafy greens.  
- **Sample 6-meal plan:**  
  1. **Breakfast:** Oats (50 g) porridge with water (190 kcal, 7 g protein) + 1 banana (~90 kcal).  
  2. **Snack:** 1 hard-boiled egg (78 kcal, 6 g protein) + 1 small fruit.  
  3. **Lunch:** 100 g cooked rice + 75 g moong dal curry (~300 kcal, 18 g protein). Veg sabzi (100 g) 50 kcal.  
  4. **Snack:** Roasted peanuts (30 g, ~170 kcal, 7 g protein) or sattu drink (50 g, 180 kcal, 10 g protein).  
  5. **Evening:** 2 chapatis (80 kcal, 6 g protein) + 100 g paneer bhurji or chicken curry (if non-veg) (~200 kcal, 15 g protein).  
  6. **Night:** 150 ml milk (full/skim) or curd (75 kcal, 5 g protein) before sleep.  

- **Budget breakdown (₹80):**  
  - Dal (moong/chana) 100 g (₹12) → 347 kcal, 24 g protein.  
  - Rice 150 g (₹9) → 195 kcal.  
  - Wheat flour 60 g (₹2) → 180 kcal, 4.8 g protein.  
  - Eggs 2 (₹12) → 156 kcal, 12 g protein.  
  - Milk 200 ml (₹10) → 70 kcal, 7 g protein.  
  - Veg 200 g (₹8) → 100 kcal.  
  - Peanuts 30 g (₹10) → 170 kcal, 7 g protein.  
  - Oil/ghee 15 g (₹3) → 135 kcal.  
  - **Total:** ~1,353 kcal, ~59 g protein (cost ~₹76).  

### 3. ₹100–200/day (₹3,000–6,000/month) – Working Professional Budget

- **Staples:** Higher quantity + variety: rice, wheat, poha, oats. Protein from pulses, eggs (3+), chicken, fish, paneer, nuts.  
- **Protein:** ~80–100 g/day.  
- **Calories:** ~2,000–2,500 kcal/day.  
- **Food stack:** Dal-rice, roti, eggs (≥2/day), 100–150 g chicken or fish, paneer/tofu, Greek yogurt, legumes, nuts, dairy milk.  
- **Sample 6-meal plan:**  
  1. **Breakfast:** Veg omelette (3 eggs, ~230 kcal,18 g protein) + 2 slices whole-wheat toast (~140 kcal, 5 g protein).  
  2. **Snack 1:** Banana (90 kcal) + peanuts (30 g, 170 kcal, 7 g protein).  
  3. **Lunch:** 150 g boiled rice (195 kcal) + 100 g chicken breast curry (~165 kcal, 31 g protein) + salad.  
  4. **Snack 2:** Lassi or curd (200 ml, 100 kcal, 7 g protein) + 1 apple.  
  5. **Evening:** 2 chapatis (160 kcal, 10 g protein) + 100 g paneer curry (~296 kcal, 18 g protein).  
  6. **Night:** Milk (200 ml, 70 kcal, 7 g protein) + a boiled egg (78 kcal, 6 g protein).  

- **Budget breakdown (₹150):**  
  - Rice 200 g (₹12) → 260 kcal.  
  - Dal (mixed) 100 g (₹12) → 347 kcal, 24 g protein.  
  - Chicken breast 150 g (₹30) → 248 kcal, 46.5 g protein.  
  - Eggs 3 (₹18) → 234 kcal, 18 g protein.  
  - Paneer 100 g (₹35) → 296 kcal, 18.3 g protein.  
  - Wheat flour 100 g (₹4) → 299 kcal, 7.9 g protein.  
  - Milk 300 ml (₹15) → 105 kcal, 10.5 g protein.  
  - Vegetables 300 g (₹12) → 150 kcal.  
  - Nuts (peanuts 30 g) ₹10 → 170 kcal, 7 g protein.  
  - Oil/ghee 20 g (₹4) → 180 kcal.  
  - **Total:** ~2,289 kcal, ~114 g protein (cost ~₹147).  

### 4. ₹200–400/day (₹6,000–12,000/month) – Comfortable Budget

- **Staples:** All of above + increased portions. More meat/fish, eggs (3–4), quality pulses (urad, rajma), mixed nuts.  
- **Protein:** ~120–150 g/day.  
- **Calories:** ~2,500–3,000 kcal.  
- **Sample plan:** Similar to budget-3 but with larger portions (e.g. 200 g chicken, 100 g fish, 100 g paneer), plus whey protein shake (120 kcal, 25 g protein).  
- **Budget breakdown:** Allow ~₹300–350/day.  

### 5. ₹400–800/day (₹12,000–24,000/month) – Premium Budget

- **Staples:** Quality foods: premium eggs, milk, meats, imported grains, organic produce. Possibly supplements (whey, fish oil).  
- **Protein:** ≥150–200 g/day.  
- **Calories:** 3,000+ kcal.  
- **Sample plan:**  
  1. Protein shake (whey 30 g, 120 kcal, 24 g protein) + fruits.  
  2. Greek yogurt + nuts.  
  3. Grilled chicken (200 g, 330 kcal, 62 g protein) + quinoa salad.  
  4. Cottage cheese or fish + wild rice.  
  5. Paneer/tofu stir-fry + chapatis.  
  6. Milk (250 ml, 90 kcal, 9 g protein) + casein or cottage cheese before bed.  

- **Budget breakdown:** Ample budget for quality: e.g. organic chicken (₹350/kg), imported salmon (₹600/kg), branded whey (₹100–150/serve) etc.

## Part C: Goal-Specific Macro Protocols & Timing

For each goal, we specify macro splits, protein (g/kg), calorie targets, food timing, and foods to avoid.

- **1. Body Recomposition (simultaneous fat loss & muscle gain; “skinny-fat” start):**  
  - **Macros:** High protein (2.2–2.5 g/kg bodyweight), moderate carbs (40–50% of kcal), moderate fats (20–30%). Example: 40% carb, 30% protein, 30% fat.  
  - **Protein:** ~2.2 g/kg (for 70 kg person → ~154 g).  
  - **Calories:** Slight deficit or maintenance. Roughly maintenance TDEE (Mifflin-St Jeor) to bodyweight (e.g. 18/M/55kg/TDEE~1796). Adjust 10% below maintenance for fat loss.  
  - **Pre-workout (1–2 h before):** Balanced small meal with carbs and protein (e.g. banana + whey shake, or oatmeal + milk).  
  - **Post-workout (within 30 min):** Fast protein (whey or egg whites) + carbs (fruit or rice).  
  - **Before bed:** Casein-rich protein (cottage cheese or milk) to preserve muscle.  
  - **Avoid:** Refined sugars, empty calories (e.g. soft drinks, deep-fried snacks) that hamper fat loss.  

- **2. Aesthetic V-Taper (wide shoulders, narrow waist, lean muscle):**  
  - **Macros:** High protein (~2.2 g/kg), moderate carbs (~45–50%), moderate fat (~20–25%). Often slightly higher carbs to fuel upper-body workouts.  
  - **Protein:** ~2.2 g/kg (maintain muscle).  
  - **Calories:** Around maintenance or slight surplus (if adding muscle) but focus on lean gain.  
  - **Timing:** Similar to recomposition, with carb emphasis around resistance training. Pre-workout: oats/fruit + egg whites; post-workout: lean protein + rice/banana. Emphasize protein distribution evenly.  
  - **Before bed:** Light protein (greek yogurt) and avoid heavy fats.  
  - **Avoid:** Excessive calories from fat; limit alcohol and high-sugar treats to prevent waist fat.  

- **3. Classic Bodybuilder Bulk:**  
  - **Macros:** High calories + high protein + high carbs. Example 50% carbs, 30% protein, 20% fat.  
  - **Protein:** 2.2–2.5 g/kg (for lean bulk).  
  - **Calories:** ~+10–15% above TDEE for gradual surplus. Eg for 70 kg: ~2,500–2,700 kcal.  
  - **Timing:**  
    - **Pre-workout:** Carb-heavy (e.g. rice/oats + protein) ~1.5–2 h before gym.  
    - **Post-workout:** Protein shake + carbs (dextrose/waxy maize) + creatine.  
    - **Before bed:** Slow protein (casein or cottage cheese).  
  - **Avoid:** Overly sugary junk that adds fat; stick to clean bulking foods (rice, potatoes, fruit, lean meats).  

- **4. Powerlifting / Strength:**  
  - **Macros:** Very high protein (2.4–2.5 g/kg) to support recovery, high carbs (55–60%), moderate fat (~20%).  
  - **Protein:** 2.4–2.5 g/kg (e.g. 80 kg lifter → ~200 g).  
  - **Calories:** ≥ maintenance (often modest surplus) to fuel training.  
  - **Timing:**  
    - **Pre-workout:** Starchy carbs + moderate protein (rice/banana + whey) ~1h prior.  
    - **During workout:** BCAAs or carb drink (optional).  
    - **Post-workout:** Whey protein + 100–150 g carbs (rice/potato) to replenish glycogen.  
    - **Bed:** Casein protein (milk, cottage cheese).  
  - **Avoid:** Excess fiber immediately pre-workout; minimize digestive discomfort.  

- **5. Weight Loss / Fat Cut (preserve muscle):**  
  - **Macros:** High protein (≥2.5 g/kg) to preserve muscle, low–moderate carbs (30–40%), moderate fat (20–30%). Example: 40% protein, 30% carbs, 30% fat.  
  - **Protein:** 2.5 g/kg or higher.  
  - **Calories:** 15–25% below maintenance.  
  - **Timing:**  
    - **Pre-workout:** Low GI carbs + protein (e.g. oats + whey 1h before).  
    - **Post-workout:** Fast protein + moderate carbs to support recovery.  
    - **Bed:** Slow-digesting protein (no heavy fats).  
  - **Avoid:** Sugary snacks, high-fructose fruits at night, alcohol, greasy fried foods.  

- **6. Endurance / Athlete Performance:**  
  - **Macros:** High carbs (60–70% of calories), moderate protein (1.6–2.0 g/kg), moderate fat (20–25%).  
  - **Protein:** 1.6–2.0 g/kg (lower end of ISSN recommendations).  
  - **Calories:** Often high (depending on training volume), up to 3,000+ kcal for distance athletes.  
  - **Timing:**  
    - **Pre-endurance event:** Carb-rich meal (rice, banana) 2–3 h before.  
    - **During:** Carbs (energy gels, banana) during prolonged exercise.  
    - **Post:** Carbs+protein (recovery shake or meal) within 30 min.  
    - **Before bed:** Carbs not necessary; light protein.  
  - **Avoid:** High-fat meals pre-workout, and heavy protein or fiber pre-run; these can cause GI distress.  

*(Protein guidelines reference: ISSN recommends ~1.4–2.0 g/kg for active individuals; athletes doing intense lifting often take ~1.6–2.0 g/kg.)*

## Part D: Kettle-Cookable Foods for Hostel Students

Using only an electric kettle (boiling water), students can prepare various simple foods. For each food below we give a method, cook time, approximate macros, cost in Kolkata, and a taste rating (1–5). 

- **Instant Oats:** Pour 50 g rolled oats into a bowl, add ~100 ml boiling water, cover 3–5 min, stir. Add fruit or milk powder for taste. *Time:* 5 min. *Macros (per serving):* ~190 kcal, 7 g protein, 30 g carbs, 3 g fat. *Cost:* ~₹5 (oats ₹80/kg). *Taste:* 4/5 (neutral, filling).  
- **Couscous:** Place 50 g couscous in bowl, pour 75 ml boiling water, cover 5 min, fluff with fork. Add salt/spices. *Time:* 5 min. *Macros:* ~180 kcal, 6 g protein, 34 g carbs, 2 g fat. *Cost:* ~₹8 (₹160/kg). *Taste:* 4/5 (wheat-like).  
- **Quinoa:** Rinse 50 g quinoa, put in bowl with 100 ml boiling water, cover 15–20 min. *Time:* 20 min. *Macros:* ~185 kcal, 6 g protein, 30 g carbs, 3 g fat. *Cost:* ~₹25 (₹500/kg). *Taste:* 4/5 (nutty).  
- **Instant Noodles (Maggi):** Add noodles block to kettle or bowl, pour boiling water to cover, cook 3–4 min, drain excess. *Time:* 5 min. *Macros:* ~350 kcal, 7 g protein, 46 g carbs, 15 g fat (per 70 g pack). *Cost:* ~₹10. *Taste:* 5/5 (tasty, but high salt/fat).  
- **Masoor Dal Soup (insta-dal):** Put 30 g red lentils in bowl, add spices, pour boiling water, simmer 10 min (keep reboiling). *Time:* 10–15 min. *Macros:* ~110 kcal, 7 g protein, 16 g carbs, 0.5 g fat. *Cost:* ₹2–3. *Taste:* 3/5 (bland unless spiced).  
- **Sattu Drink:** Mix 30 g sattu (roasted gram flour) with salt/spices in bowl, add 150 ml boiled water, stir. *Time:* 2 min. *Macros:* ~115 kcal, 7 g protein, 18 g carbs, 1.2 g fat. *Cost:* ₹1 (₹37/kg). *Taste:* 3/5 (earthy).  
- **Egg (Sous-vide style):** Crack 1 egg into a sealed ziplock (poke small hole to vent air), submerge bag in boiling water 8–10 min. *Time:* 10 min. *Macros:* 78 kcal, 6 g protein, 0.6 g carbs, 5.5 g fat. *Cost:* ₹6. *Taste:* 4/5 (hard-boiled). (*Caution:* ensure bag doesn’t touch coil.)  
- **Peanut Butter Milk:** Mix 2 tbsp peanut butter with 200 ml hot water/milk for a shake. *Time:* 2 min. *Macros:* ~190 kcal, 8 g protein, 6 g carbs, 16 g fat. *Cost:* ₹8 (PB ₹400/kg). *Taste:* 5/5 (rich, sweet).  
- **Soup (Knorr Mug Soups):** Empty powder into mug, add boiling water, stir. *Time:* 2 min. *Macros:* ~60 kcal, 1 g protein, 8 g carbs. *Cost:* ₹10 per sachet. *Taste:* 3/5 (salty).  
- **Instant Poha (Flattened rice):** Soak 50 g poha in boiling water 5 min, drain and season. *Time:* 5 min. *Macros:* ~175 kcal, 4 g protein, 38 g carbs, 0.5 g fat. *Cost:* ₹2 (₹40/kg). *Taste:* 4/5 (light, cereal-like).  

These kettle recipes offer protein-carbs balance with minimal kitchen gear. Costs assume July 2026 Kolkata rates.  

## Part E: Body-Type Caloric Calculators

**Mifflin–St Jeor TDEE formula:** 

- **Male:** BMR = 10×W + 6.25×H – 5×Age + 5.  
- **Female:** BMR = 10×W + 6.25×H – 5×Age – 161.  
Then **TDEE = BMR × ActivityFactor**, where ActivityFactor = 1.2 (sedentary), 1.375 (light), 1.55 (moderate), 1.725 (very active).  

**Examples:**  
1. **18-y male, 55 kg, 165 cm, sedentary (AF=1.2):**  
   BMR = 10×55 + 6.25×165 – 5×18 + 5 = 550 + 1031.25 – 90 + 5 = 1496 kcal.  
   TDEE = 1496×1.2 = **≈1796 kcal/day**.  

2. **22-y male, 68 kg, 170 cm, moderately active (1.55):**  
   BMR = 10×68 + 6.25×170 – 5×22 +5 = 680 + 1062.5 – 110 + 5 = 1637.5 kcal.  
   TDEE = 1637.5×1.55 = **≈2538 kcal**.  

3. **25-y female, 55 kg, 158 cm, lightly active (1.375):**  
   BMR = 10×55 + 6.25×158 – 5×25 –161 = 550 + 987.5 – 125 – 161 = 1251.5 kcal.  
   TDEE = 1251.5×1.375 = **≈1721 kcal**.  

4. **30-y male, 80 kg, 175 cm, very active (1.725):**  
   BMR = 10×80 + 6.25×175 – 5×30 +5 = 800 + 1093.75 – 150 +5 = 1748.75 kcal.  
   TDEE = 1748.75×1.725 = **≈3017 kcal**.  

*(Worked examples use standard activity multipliers.)*

## Part G: Dietary Restrictions – Adapted Meal Plans

Below are guidelines and example adjustments for special diets, using the same budget frameworks. 

- **Pure Vegetarians (no eggs/meat, but dairy/eggs allowed) – “Lacto-vegetarian”:**  
  - *Protein sources:* Dal, paneer, yogurt, milk, nuts, soya (tofu, soya chunks), legumes.  
  - *Calories/protein:* Slightly lower than omnivores; add paneer/tofu extra to hit protein.  
  - *Examples:* Substitute eggs with paneer bhurji or curd; add 50 g paneer (100 kcal, 10 g protein) per meal.  
  - *Avoid:* No meat/fish; ensure B12 from dairy or supplement.  
  - *Meal plan:* Similar to budget plans but omit meat. For moderate budget, e.g., **Lunch:** chana masala + rice + 100 g paneer curry. **Snacks:** greek yogurt, roasted chana, nut butter.  

- **Vegans (no animal products at all):**  
  - *Protein sources:* Lentils, beans, tofu, soya milk, nuts/seeds, whole grains, spirulina.  
  - *Calories/protein:* Use larger servings (e.g. 75–100 g lentils per meal) and combine foods for complete protein.  
  - *Examples:* Use tofu (firm) instead of paneer; almond/soy milk instead of dairy; sattu and peanut butter are vegan staples.  
  - *Avoid:* All dairy, eggs, honey.  
  - *Supplements:* B12 and D recommended.  
  - *Meal plan:* At ₹100/day: **Breakfast:** oats with soy milk + flaxseeds; **Lunch:** rajma + brown rice + sautéed greens (spinach); **Snack:** peanut butter on toast; **Dinner:** stir-fried tofu + roti.  

- **Lactose Intolerance (no lactose/dairy):**  
  - *Protein:* Similar to vegetarians but no milk, paneer, curd. Use whey isolate (if tolerated) or plant proteins.  
  - *Calcium:* Use fortified soy milk, tofu, leafy greens.  
  - *Meals:* Replace dairy with soy/yoghurt alternatives. E.g., milk→soy milk, curd→soy yogurt, paneer→tofu. Eggs and meat (if non-veg) allowed.  

- **Halal Diet (no pork, alcohol):**  
  - *Protein:* All allowed meats except pork. Chicken, mutton, fish must be halal-sourced. Dairy and eggs are fine.  
  - *Meals:* Same structure as budgets. E.g. for moderate budget: **Lunch:** halal chicken curry + rice; **Snacks:** eggs, nuts, fruits.  
  - *Avoid:* Pork products; ensure gelatin supplements (like some whey/multivitamins) are halal-certified.  
  - *Supplement note:* Choose halal-certified whey/creatine if concerned; otherwise, standard products suffice.  

Each restricted diet follows the same 6-meal frameworks above but swaps in allowed foods. Calories and protein are maintained by careful substitution (e.g. more legumes/nuts for vegans, paneer for vegetarians). These adaptations keep budget and macro targets similar to Part B frameworks, just replacing disallowed foods with equivalents.
