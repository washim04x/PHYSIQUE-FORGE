**Technique Steps, Mistakes & Cues (examples):** 

- **Shrug (Dumbbell):** Stand upright holding dumbbells by sides. Step 1: Feet hip-width, core braced. Step 2: Without bending arms, lift shoulders straight up as if “shrugging at ears.” Step 3: Hold peak contraction (1–2s) squeezing traps. Step 4: Lower shoulders slowly. (*Common mistakes:* rolling shoulders forward/back, bending elbows). *Cue:* “Lift shoulders straight up – imagine pushing them toward your ears.”  
- **Face Pull:** Step 1: Attach rope at high pulley. Step 2: Grip rope ends (palms facing each other) and step back. Step 3: Starting tall, pull rope toward your forehead, flaring elbows wide, squeezing shoulder blades. Step 4: Pause and return slowly. (*Mistakes:* leaning back too far, letting elbows drop). *Cue:* “Pull apart the rope, elbows out and back.”  
- **Upright Row:** Step 1: Grip barbell at shoulder width with overhand grip. Step 2: Drive elbows straight up to shoulder level, keeping bar close to body. Step 3: Pause at top (elbows just above shoulders), then lower under control. (*Mistakes:* lifting too high (stop at chin level), rounding shoulders). *Cue:* “Lead with elbows, keep them higher than wrists.”  
- **Rack Pull:** Step 1: Set bar at knee height on a rack. Stand so bar near shins, feet hip-width. Step 2: Hinge hips back, grab bar slightly wider than shoulders. Step 3: Brace core, pull through legs by extending hips, keeping back flat. Step 4: Squeeze traps at lockout. (*Mistakes:* jerking; rounding lower back). *Cue:* “Pretend to ‘wrap’ bar around legs and stand tall.”

# Part B: Goal-Specific Training Programs

Below are example periodized programs for each goal, including splits, weekly schedules, phases (progression over weeks), daily exercise plans with sets×reps×rest, progressive overload rules, advanced techniques, exercises to avoid, and deload strategies.

---

## 1. Aesthetic V-Taper (Wide Shoulders/Back, Narrow Waist)

- **Focus:** Build shoulder width (lateral delts, traps), lat width, and keep waist lean. **Avoid heavy oblique or anterior core loading** (e.g. heavy barbell squats, deadlifts) that increase intra-abdominal pressure and may “thicken” the midsection.
- **Training Split:** 4–5 days with emphasis on upper body width:
  - **Example Split:**  
    - Monday: Shoulders (primary) + Upper Back (lats)  
    - Tuesday: Lower Body (glutes/hamstrings focus, minimal lumbar flexion)  
    - Wednesday: Rest or Active Recovery (light mobility/stretch)  
    - Thursday: Shoulders (again) + Lats/Upper Back  
    - Friday: Chest/Arms (light on abs)  
    - Saturday: Optional Full Back + Core (light)  
    - Sunday: Rest  

- **Weekly Schedule:** E.g., M: Pull/Shoulder, T: Legs, W: Rest, Th: Pull/Shoulder, F: Push, Sa: Full Back/Core, Su: Rest.
- **Phase Structure:**  
  - *Phase 1 (Weeks 1–4):* Foundation (moderate volume). Focus on mastering technique and moderate weights; avoid muscle imbalances.  
  - *Phase 2 (Weeks 5–8):* Progressive overload (increase weight or volume). Introduce intensity techniques (e.g. drop sets on last set of flyes or lateral raises).  
  - *Phase 3 (Weeks 9–12):* Advanced hypertrophy (higher volume/intensity). Add supersets and rest-pause on accessory work.  

- **Daily Workouts (example):**  
  - *Monday (Shoulders/Back):*  
    1. Barbell Overhead Press – 4×6–8 (90 s)  
    2. Pull-Ups (wide grip) – 4×6–10 (90 s)  
    3. Dumbbell Lateral Raise – 3×12–15 (60 s)  
    4. Cable Face Pull – 3×12–15 (60 s)  
    5. Straight-Arm Pulldown – 3×12–15 (60 s)  
    6. Plank – 3×45–60 s (–)  

  - *Thursday (Shoulders/Back):*  
    1. Dumbbell Arnold Press – 4×8–10 (90 s)  
    2. Lat Pulldown (wide grip) – 4×8–12 (90 s)  
    3. Seated Cable Row – 3×8–12 (60 s)  
    4. Dumbbell Rear Delt Fly – 3×12–15 (60 s)  
    5. Hammer Curl – 3×10–12 (60 s)  
    6. Pallof Press – 3×10–12 (each side) (30 s)  

  - *Saturday (Optional Chest/Back/Core):*  
    1. Incline Bench Press – 3×8–12 (90 s)  
    2. T-Bar Row – 3×8–12 (90 s)  
    3. Dumbbell Fly – 2×12–15 (60 s)  
    4. Cable Pull-Through – 3×10–12 (60 s)  
    5. Ab Wheel Rollout – 3×8–10 (60 s)  

  (*Leg days focus on posterior chain and glutes, avoiding heavy squats. For example, Romanian Deadlift, Bulgarian Split Squat, Leg Curl, Glute Bridge, and light calf work.*)

- **Progressive Overload:**  
  - *Phase 1:* Double progression – begin at lower rep (e.g. 6) and add reps each week until top (e.g. 10–12), then increase weight.  
  - *Phase 2:* Linear weight progression on compound lifts (e.g. +2.5–5% each week) while maintaining rep range. Use RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion) ~7–8 in early sets.  
  - *Phase 3:* Increase total volume (more sets or exercises) and use drop sets/giant sets on last sets of some exercises.  

- **Advanced Techniques:** Supersets (e.g. shoulder press + lateral raise), drop sets on lateral raises or pull-downs, rest-pause on final push-up set, partial reps for wide grip pull-down, mechanical drop (e.g. wide-grip pull-ups to chin-ups). EMOM push-ups for conditioning and core stability.  
- **Avoid:** Heavy barbell squats and deadlifts (they increase intra-abdominal pressure and can “thicken” the waist). Direct oblique-loading exercises (e.g. heavy side bends, twisting crunches) – stick to front-only core moves or anti-rotation drills. As noted by experts, excessive oblique work counteracts a narrow waist aesthetic. Instead, use vacuum exercises and planks for a cinched waist. Heavy overhead press can also widen ribs/trunk; use moderate loads and strict technique.  
- **Deload Strategy:** Every 6–8 weeks take a deload week (reduce volume 50% or intensity 40–60%). For example, cut weights by ~50% but do same exercises with light reps, or halve sets for upper body. Light cardio/stretching can replace heavy lifts. According to ACSM recommendations, even bodybuilders should deload roughly every 6–8 weeks to avoid overtraining.  

*(Sources: ACSM guidelines recommend heavy loads for strength (~80%1RM) and ample volume for hypertrophy. Avoiding oblique hypertrophy is advised for V-taper aesthetics. Sweeping evidence shows training shoulders/back twice weekly is effective for growth.)*

## 2. Classic Bodybuilder (Symmetrical Mass)

- **Focus:** Maximize overall muscle hypertrophy with balanced development. Train all muscle groups with variety and high volume. Include both compound and isolation exercises.  
- **Training Split:** 5- or 6-day “bro-split” or upper/lower split to target muscle groups twice per week.  
  - **Example (6-day Push/Pull/Legs):**  
    - Mon: Chest + Abs  
    - Tue: Back + Calves  
    - Wed: Legs (Quads/Glutes) + Abs  
    - Thu: Shoulders + Traps + Abs  
    - Fri: Arms (Biceps/Triceps) + Calves  
    - Sat: Full Body or Weak Point focus + Ab work  
    - Sun: Rest/Active Recovery  

- **Weekly Schedule:** E.g., PPL/rest, repeating twice (6 days) then 1 rest.  
- **Phase Structure:**  
  - *Phase 1 (Weeks 1–4):* Moderate load (8–12 rep range) with high volume (~3–4 exercises per muscle). Establish technique and endurance.  
  - *Phase 2 (Weeks 5–8):* Increase weight or volume (e.g. +1 set per exercise) for progressive overload. Introduce supersets (antagonistic like chest-back) and drop sets on last set of isolation movements.  
  - *Phase 3 (Weeks 9–12):* Peak hypertrophy – use advanced techniques (giant sets, rest-pause) on lagging body parts, vary rep schemes (6–12).  

- **Daily Workouts (example)**:  
  - *Monday (Chest + Abs):* Bench Press 4×8–12; Incline Dumbbell Press 3×8–12; Pec Deck Fly 3×12–15; Cable Crossover 3×12–15; Hanging Leg Raise 3×15–20.  
  - *Tuesday (Back + Calves):* Deadlift 4×5–8; Pull-Up 4×8–12; Barbell Row 3×8–12; Seated Cable Row 3×8–12; Standing Calf Raise 4×15–20.  
  - *Wednesday (Legs + Abs):* Barbell Squat 4×8–12; Leg Press 3×12–15; Lying Leg Curl 3×10–15; Bulgarian Split Squat 3×10 each; Plank 3×60 s.  
  - *Thursday (Shoulders/Traps + Abs):* Overhead Press 4×8–12; Lateral Raise 3×12–15; Face Pull 3×12–15; Dumbbell Shrug 3×10–12; Cable Crunch 3×12–15.  
  - *Friday (Arms + Calves):* Close-Grip Bench Press 3×8–12; EZ-Bar Curl 3×8–12; Skullcrusher 3×10–12; Hammer Curls 3×10–12; Seated Calf Raise 4×15–20.  
  - *Saturday (Weak Point/Full Body):* e.g. Chest flyes, rows, ab-wheel, curls, hip thrust – targeting any lagging areas with 3–4 exercises.  

- **Progressive Overload:** Weekly or bi-weekly increases in weight or reps. Use double progression (hit upper rep range before adding load). Log weights and strive to beat past performance. RPE should reach ~8–9 on final sets. Vary set/rep schemes (periodize 12→8 rep ranges over phases).  
- **Advanced Techniques:** Common in bodybuilding: **Supersets** (agonist/antagonist, e.g. biceps/triceps back-to-back), **Giant Sets** (3+ exercises for same muscle, e.g. chest press→fly→push-up). **Drop Sets** on last set of isolation (dumbbell flies, cable curls) to fully fatigue muscle. **Rest-Pause** sets (brief rests to squeeze extra reps). **Partial reps and burns** at failure. EMOM training occasionally (e.g. 1 chin-up/min) for conditioning.  
- **Avoid:** In general bodybuilding has no strict forbiddances, but safety: avoid ego-lifting (e.g. extremely heavy deadlifts with bad form). Also minimize excessive cardio that can impede muscle gain.  
- **Deload:** Every 4–8 weeks reduce load or volume. For example, take one week to cut sets in half or use ~50% normal weight (as recommended by ACSM practice). Listen to fatigue; signs like persistent soreness or strength loss mean a deload week is due.

*(ACSM guidelines endorse ≥10 weekly sets per muscle group for hypertrophy, consistent with high-volume bodybuilding splits. Advanced techniques boost metabolic stress (some evidence drop sets can increase hypertrophy).)*

## 3. Powerlifting / Strength (Squat, Bench, Deadlift Focus)

- **Focus:** Maximize 1RM strength in squat, bench, deadlift. Secondary aim to build overall power and muscle mass. Minimal isolation work; primary on heavy compound lifts.  
- **Training Split:** 3–4 day split concentrating on main lifts multiple times/week. Common splits:  
  - **Example Split (4-day Upper/Lower):**  
    - Monday: Squat-focused lower body  
    - Tuesday: Bench-focused upper body  
    - Thursday: Deadlift-focused lower body  
    - Friday: Overhead Press/Bench upper body  
    - (Wed/Sat/Sun: Rest or light cardio/recovery)  

- **Weekly Schedule:** M: Squat, T: Bench, W: Rest, Th: Deadlift, F: Press, Sa/Su: Rest.  
- **Phase Structure:** Often linear or periodized by 8–12 week blocks. Example:  
  - *Phase 1 (Weeks 1–4):* Volume phase with moderate loads (e.g. 3–5 sets of 5–6 @ ~75%1RM) focusing on technique and accumulating work.  
  - *Phase 2 (Weeks 5–8):* Intensity phase, heavier loads (4–6×2–4 @ 80–90%1RM) for strength.  
  - *Phase 3 (Weeks 9–12):* Peaking phase, singles/doubles at near-PR weights (e.g. 4×1–3 @ 90–95%1RM).  

- **Daily Workouts (example):**  
  - *Monday (Squat Day):*  
    1. Barbell Back Squat – 4×5 @ 75%1RM (120 s)  
    2. Pause Squats – 2×3–5 (paused 2s at bottom) (90 s)  
    3. Leg Press – 3×8–10 (90 s)  
    4. Plank – 3×60 s (–)  

  - *Tuesday (Bench Day):*  
    1. Barbell Bench Press – 4×5 @ 75%1RM (120 s)  
    2. Close-Grip Bench Press – 3×5–8 (90 s)  
    3. Barbell Row – 3×6–8 (90 s)  
    4. Hanging Leg Raise – 3×10 (60 s)  

  - *Thursday (Deadlift Day):*  
    1. Deadlift – 4×3 @ 80%1RM (120–180 s)  
    2. Romanian Deadlift – 3×6–8 (90 s)  
    3. Hamstring Curl – 3×8–12 (60 s)  
    4. Back Extension – 3×10–12 (60 s)  

  - *Friday (Press Day):*  
    1. Overhead Press – 4×5 (90 s)  
    2. Bench Press (Paused or Spoto) – 3×5 (90 s)  
    3. Chin-Up – 3×6–10 (90 s)  
    4. Farmer’s Walk – 2×30–45 s (–)  

- **Progressive Overload:** For powerlifting, linear load increases each session/week on main lifts (e.g. +5 lbs/squat, +5 lbs/bench, +10 lbs/dead per week). Or use **percentage-based** loading (e.g. Squat @ 70%1RM ×3, next week 75%×3, etc.) with planned RPE. Track and aim to break PRs. Typically **RPE/RIR** is used to auto-regulate intensity, aiming for 1–2 rep max sets (RPE9–10). See ACSM: strength focus ~80%1RM and low reps.  
- **Advanced Techniques:** Limited in pure strength programs, but some may use *RPE clusters* (e.g. perform single reps with 20–30 s rest to accumulate volume) or *wave loading* (varying reps 3-2-1 each workout). 3-sec eccentrics can build control on assistance lifts. Use occasional speed sets (50–60% 1RM x 2–3 explosively) on squats/bench to train power.  
- **Avoid:** Because goal is strength, avoid high-rep fatigue sets that may impair recovery. Also avoid hypertrophy-only isolation that detract from main lifts (e.g. cable flyes). Keep accessory work purposeful (e.g. tricep extensions, lat pull-downs to strengthen bench/dead).  
- **Deload:** Power athletes often deload every 4–6 weeks or before a meet. A deload week includes 60% intensity with volume reduced by ~50%. For example, perform main lifts at ~50–60% weight for few reps, or cut sets in half, focusing on technique and recovery.

## 4. Calisthenics / Bodyweight Only (No Gym)

- **Focus:** Build strength and muscle using only bodyweight and minimal equipment (bars, rings). Emphasize pull-ups, push-ups, dips, and progression variations.  
- **Training Split:** 3–4 day split mixing push, pull, and legs, with skill practice (handstands). Example 4-day:  
  - Day 1: Pull (back, biceps)  
  - Day 2: Push (chest, triceps, shoulders) + core  
  - Day 3: Rest / Active mobility  
  - Day 4: Leg Day (bodyweight squats, lunges, jumps)  
  - Day 5: Core + Handstand work  
  - Day 6–7: Rest / flexibility  

- **Progressions:** Each exercise has easier → harder versions: e.g. negative pull-ups → assisted pull-ups → full pull-ups → weighted pull-ups; knee push-ups → incline push-ups → push-ups → decline/one-arm push-up; assisted handstand → freestanding.  
- **Sample Exercises:** Pull-Ups, Australian Rows, Chin-Ups, Inverted Rows; Dips (parallettes/chairs), Push-Ups (various angles), Pike Push-Ups/Handstand Push-Ups; Pistols (one-leg squats), Jump Squats, Nordic Curls; Planks, L-Sits, Dragon Flags for abs.  
- **Example Full Program (3 days):**  
  - *Day A (Pull/Core):* Australian Row 4×8–12; Pull-Up (assisted/progress) 4×5–10; Chin-Up 3×6–10; Hanging Leg Raise 3×10; Plank 3×60 s.  
  - *Day B (Push/Core):* Push-Up 4×10–15; Dips (chair or parallel) 4×6–12; Pike Push-Up 3×8–10; Diamond Push-Up 2×12; Hollow Hold 3×30 s.  
  - *Day C (Legs/Core):* Bodyweight Squat or Bulgarian Split 4×10–15; Pistol Squat progression 3×5–8; Nordic Ham Curl 3×5–8; Calf Raise (single-leg) 3×12; L-Sit 3×20–30 s.  

  *Alternate push/pull/legs/core across 5–6 days for variety.*  
- **Progressive Overload:** Increase reps, add holds (pauses at bottom/top), increase lever length (harder push-up variations), or add weight (backpack). For strength-endurance, use circuits or EMOM (e.g. 5 push-ups every minute).  
- **Advanced Techniques:** *Archer push-ups, clapping push-ups* (plyometric), *muscle-ups* (advanced pull). Use supersets (e.g. pull/push superset). *Dropsets* via angle adjustment (e.g. do push-ups then immediately do knee push-ups). EMOM or AMRAP sets to push volume.  
- **Exercises to Avoid:** None specifically off-limits; but overweight individuals may strain joints with high-rep plyo initially. Modify instead.  

*(Note: ACSM now acknowledges bodyweight training is highly effective. Focus on technique and gradual progression.)*

## 5. Weight Loss / Fat Burning (Muscle-Preserving)

- **Focus:** Maximize caloric burn and preserve muscle. Combine resistance training with metabolic conditioning. Moderate caloric deficit, high-protein diet.  
- **Training Split:** Full-body or upper/lower circuits 3–5x/week. Emphasize compound lifts and circuit training.  
  - **Example 4-day Upper/Lower Split:**  
    - Monday: Full Body Strength  
    - Tuesday: HIIT or MetCon (bodyweight/cardio)  
    - Thursday: Full Body Strength  
    - Friday: HIIT/MetCon or Rest  
    - Sat/Sun: Active recovery (light cardio, mobility)  

- **Weekly Schedule:** e.g., M: Strength, T: HIIT, W: Rest, Th: Strength, F: HIIT, Sa: Active, Su: Rest.  
- **Phase Structure:** 8–12 week blocks. Intensity-based with some variation:  
  - *Phase 1:* Building base strength (3–4 sets of 8–12 on big lifts) and moderate-intensity cardio (20–30 min steady-state).  
  - *Phase 2:* Introduce circuits/supersets to elevate heart rate (e.g., bench→row superset). Start interval training (e.g., 30s sprint/30s rest ×10).  
  - *Phase 3:* Emphasize higher frequency (4–5 days) with mixed lifting and metabolic sets. Include advanced techniques (drop sets, active rest) to increase EPOC.  

- **Daily Workouts:**  
  - *Strength Days (Full Body):* Squat 3×8–10; Bench Press 3×8–10; Bent-Over Row 3×8–10; Overhead Press 3×8–10; Deadlift or Hip Thrust 3×6–8; Plank 3×60 s.  
  - *Circuit Days:* Cycle through 5–6 exercises with minimal rest (e.g., Pull-Up, Push-Up, Jump Squat, Dumbbell Row, Burpees) for 3–4 rounds.  
  - *HIIT:* Sprint intervals (e.g., 10× 30 s sprint/30 s rest) or Tabata bodyweight (push-up, squat, plank).  

- **Progressive Overload:** Even in fat-loss phase, apply double-progression on strength lifts. Track performance (use log or RPE). Increase total volume (e.g. add a set each week) or intensity (less rest).  
- **Advanced Techniques:** **Drop sets, rest-pause, supersets** are especially useful to increase caloric burn (short rests increases EPOC). **Compound supersets** (push/pull exercises back-to-back) maximize efficiency. EMOM circuits (e.g. 5 pull-ups at each minute on the minute for 10 min).  
- **Avoid:** Long steady-state cardio at the expense of lifting; overtraining deficit causing muscle loss. Limit isolation exercises (they burn fewer calories).  
- **Deload:** Similar to others, schedule a light week every 6–8 weeks. Use it for active recovery (yoga, swimming) and fewer lifts. Watch for signs of plateau or excessive fatigue.  

*(E.g., ACSM suggests mixing strength and cardiovascular exercise for fat loss. Maintaining high protein and performing resistance training helps **preserve muscle** during a calorie deficit.)*

## 6. Beginner General Fitness (First-Time Gym, No Experience)

- **Focus:** Learn basic movements, build habit, improve strength and endurance safely. Emphasize full-body, lower loads, higher reps for technique practice.  
- **Training Split:** 3× full-body per week (e.g., M/W/F). Use full-body routines to maximize frequency for all muscles.  
- **Weekly Schedule:** Mon/Wed/Fri: Training; other days rest or light activity.  
- **Phase Structure:**  
  - *Phase 1 (Weeks 1–4):* Foundation – 2–3 sets of 12–15 reps, light weights focusing on form. Full body each session (squat, push, pull, core, etc.).  
  - *Phase 2 (Weeks 5–8):* Progressive – 3–4 sets
